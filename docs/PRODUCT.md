# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Independent developers and frontend learners, especially practical “vibe coders,” use the app during focused study sessions on desktop or mobile. They want to understand React and Next.js well enough to build, debug, review, and explain production code.

## Product Purpose

Learn Next is a local-first learning platform that turns source-backed lessons into a loop of learning, practice, feedback, confirmation, and review. Success means a learner can start the next useful topic quickly, explain why it matters, submit an answer, understand mistakes, and retain the skill over time.

## Brand Personality

Calm, practical, encouraging. The voice is a smart friend explaining technical ideas clearly, with specific reasoning and no unnecessary jargon. The interface should make thinking and feedback feel safe, focused, and earned.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic course catalogs, engagement-first gamification, decorative dashboards, vague “correct/incorrect” feedback, and interfaces that make reading look complete while hiding the practice or submission step. Avoid emoji-only meaning, inaccessible custom controls, and split product identities such as calling a React + Next.js platform only “Learn React.”

## Design Principles

- Make the next learning action obvious.
- Keep explanation, practice, submission, and feedback in one coherent flow.
- Show why a recommendation or result exists.
- Prefer durable understanding over completion theater.
- Keep learner data local, private, and exportable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard-only completion, visible focus, semantic landmarks, labelled controls, screen-reader status feedback, 44px touch targets, readable contrast, reduced motion, high contrast, text-size preferences, and no horizontal overflow at 320px.
